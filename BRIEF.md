# Brief: salmon-run

**Round:** 18
**Protagonist:** Gary — an ordinary sockeye salmon on his once-in-a-lifetime upstream journey. He is not fast. He is not smart. He is absolutely going to make it.
**Mechanic verb:** DODGE / LEAP
**One line:** Gary the salmon fights upstream through rapids, grizzly bears, and fishing lines — tap to dodge left and right, hold to charge a leap over waterfalls.

**Backstory:** Every autumn, Gary swims upstream from the ocean to spawn. He has done exactly zero of this before. The river is full of rocks, hungry bears, and one very determined fisherman named Doug. Gary doesn't think about any of this. Gary just swims.

**World:** A Pacific Northwest river in peak autumn — golden maples, white water rapids, mossy boulders, a grizzly bear knee-deep in the shallows. Everything is beautiful and trying to eat Gary.

**Mechanic:**
- Auto-scroll (river current pushes Gary back, he swims forward)
- 3 lanes: tap left / tap right to dodge between them
- Hold to charge leap for waterfalls — release to jump (timing matters)
- Obstacles: boulders (dodge), grizzly bear paws (dodge fast), fishing lines (duck under = tap down or just time lane), waterfalls (hold-charge leap)

**Background:** Rich river green `#3D7A52` — forest reflected in moving water
**BPM:** 132, heroic / slightly absurd orchestral adventure

**Music direction:** Like a nature documentary score written by someone who takes Gary extremely seriously. Triumphant French horns. Dramatic timpani. A piccolo melody that sounds almost like a theme song. But the moment you die, it deflates into a cartoon tuba. The music BELIEVES in Gary even when Gary doesn't deserve it.
